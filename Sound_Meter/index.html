Logarithmic scale

Every +20 dB = ×10 more amplitude. So −20 dBFS is 10× quieter than 0 dBFS, and −60 dBFS is 1,000× quieter. That's why the dB scale feels "stretched" at the bottom.

Noise floor & S/N ratio

The noise floor is the baseline hiss from electronics and the room. The S/N ratio is the gap between your signal and that floor — aim for 60 dB or more for clean audio.

Clipping & distortion

At 0 dBFS, digital audio hits a hard ceiling. The waveform is sliced flat, producing harsh harmonic distortion. Unlike analog tape, digital clip sounds ugly — keep peaks below −3 dBFS.
